But the reconciled `StatisticalLearning.jsx` prose does not match what the real computation actually does, in two independently verifiable ways:

1. **"bias² fall[s]" through "roughly 8" is false for most of that range.** The lede (`:358-368`) says: "Drag the complexity slider from 1 toward roughly 8 while watching bias² fall and variance rise." I recomputed `bias2(d, noiseLevel)` for `d=1..12` at noise 0.1, 0.4, and 1.0 (all three, to rule out a default-noise-only artifact). In every case bias² falls only from d=1 to a shallow minimum around d=3-5, then *rises* — first gently, then explosively — for the rest of the 1-8 range: at noise=0.4, bias2(d=3)=0.0027 but bias2(d=8)=29.4, a ~10,000× increase; at noise=0.1, bias2(d=6)=0.00098 but bias2(d=8)=1.84, a ~1,900× increase. So a reader following the widget's own instruction ("drag from 1 toward 8 while watching bias² fall") would observe bias² *rising* dramatically for roughly the back half of that range, the opposite of the claim.
2. **The noise→optimal-complexity shift is not observable across most of the described range.** Both the lede and `tryThis` (`:371-378`) claim raising the noise level shifts "the total-error minimum — the optimal-complexity marker — toward simpler models," framed as something to drag-and-notice across the slider. I swept `findOptimalComplexity(noiseLevel)` across the widget's actual slider steps (0.10 to 1.00 in 0.05 increments) and the optimum is pinned at d=3 for every noise value from 0.10 through 0.90 — 17 of the 19 slider positions — only dropping to d=1 at noise=0.95 and 1.00. A reader dragging the noise slider through its first ~90% of range would see no shift at all in the "Optimal d★" panel.

Both effects are structural/deterministic given the fixed seeds (`1000+b`), not Monte Carlo noise from an under-sampled estimate — re-running confirms the same shape at every noise level tested.
