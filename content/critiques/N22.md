## REQUIRED

1. **`src/pages/ch22/EvaluatingLLMsAndAgents.jsx` — perplexity is incorrectly called a proper scoring rule.** The proper scoring rule is the mean negative log score (cross-entropy), not its exponent. A monotone transform preserves rankings on one fixed realized corpus, but it does not preserve propriety under expectation. For a one-token Bernoulli event with true probability `q`, expected perplexity is `q/p + (1-q)/(1-p)`, whose minimizer is generally not `p=q` (for `q=0.8`, it is about `p=0.667`). Replace the sentence with the precise claim that token-average negative log loss is strictly proper and perplexity is its exponential/effective branching-factor presentation.
2. **`src/components/widgets/ch22/JudgeAgreementExplorer.jsx` and its page hand-off — the widget does not implement the displayed logistic measurement model and its directed interaction does not reliably produce the promised aggregate change.** The page defines `P(A \succ B) = sigma(margin)`, but the widget never evaluates `sigma`; it deterministically thresholds the raw margin at zero. It then calls the fraction of those thresholded choices an observed win fraction. The simplification is not disclosed. In addition, following the natural endpoint of the instruction gives position `30`, verbosity `30`, agreement `100%`, and Elo-style rating `1000`, exactly the defaults; at position `30`, verbosity `0`, two pair outcomes flip but the aggregate also remains `1000`. Implement the stated probability model and a faithful expected/sampled aggregation, or explicitly define the zero-threshold toy model in the page and widget, then choose data/presets whose directed action visibly and robustly produces the promised ranking change.

Running an evaluator is not the same as satisfying it, especially in a chapter teaching outcome validity. Make the canned trace disclose an actual passing test result (or compute outcome from an explicit result) before awarding `1.00 pass`.
